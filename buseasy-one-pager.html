Smarter bus booking for passengers, operators, and admins.

BusEasy is a role-based bus reservation platform that helps passengers find dated trips, lets operators publish and manage departures, and gives admins a clear approval and monitoring console.

04 What happens after departure
Trips close automatically, then remain visible as history
Lifecycle path
draft → published → completed

Once a trip’s departure time passes, the backend marks it completed on a scheduled job so it no longer behaves like a live departure. Seat holds clear automatically and booking actions stop.

Draft stays editable Published is bookable Completed becomes history
Role matrix
Passenger / Operator / Admin

Passenger

Past trips stay in booking history and receipts, but seat selection and cancellations stop once the trip has passed.

Operator

Completed trips are read-only for core data, but operators can still inspect manifests and update status before closure when needed.

Admin

Admins keep full oversight over historical trips and can apply manual overrides for exceptional cases.

06 Role-by-role use cases
Who uses it and what they do

Passenger

Find a trip, inspect fare and availability, select seats, book, and manage that booking later.

  • Search by origin, destination, and date.
  • Choose one or more seats with passenger names.
  • Review live trip details before confirming.
  • See booking references and cancel confirmed bookings.

Operator

Create the supply that passengers book against, then operate trips with clearer controls and visibility.

  • Add vehicles and route templates.
  • Create draft or published trips from defaults.
  • Open manifests, see occupancy, and update trip status.
  • Use alerts for drafts, departing-soon trips, and low occupancy.

Admin

Approve operators and keep an eye on platform health without bouncing between multiple views.

  • Review pending operator accounts.
  • Approve or reject access.
  • Monitor active, inactive, and empty-trips context.
  • Track bookings and supply-side health in one console.

07 How the flow works
A simple end-to-end journey
1. Enter the platform
The landing page directs users to the right role and login path, so passengers, operators, and admins land in different experiences immediately.
2. Create supply
Operators create vehicles and routes first, then use those defaults to draft or publish trips with fares, dates, and times.
3. Book live seats
Passengers open the coach map, pick seats with clear open/sold/on-hold states, enter passenger names, and confirm before the 5-minute hold expires.
4. Monitor and approve
Admins review pending operators, monitor platform health, and keep the supply side trustworthy while operators manage active trips.
08 Tech stack
Simple architecture, role-based control
Frontend React + Vite single-page app with role-based routes and reusable workspace components.
Backend Express API with role guards, booking logic, operator approval, and trip management endpoints.
Database MongoDB models for users, vehicles, route templates, trips, and bookings.
Security JWT auth, approval gating, and server-side validation for seats, roles, and setup inputs.
